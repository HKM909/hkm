  1. CHICKEN CURRY

    2. Procedure:
    Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, mix the chicken with yogurt, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight in the fridge for better flavor). Cook the Onions: Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add Ginger, Garlic, and Chilies: Stir in minced ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Mix in the tomato puree, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the mixture. Cook the Chicken: Add the marinated chicken to the pan. Stir well to coat the chicken with the spices. Cover and cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Finish the Curry: Once the chicken is cooked through, add garam masala and mix well. Adjust salt and add water if you prefer a thinner curry. Let it simmer for another 5 minutes. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with rice or naan.

  2. GULAB JAMUN

    3. Combine Ingredients: In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil over medium heat. Add Flavor: Once the sugar dissolves, add cardamom powder and rose water. Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes. The syrup should be slightly sticky but not too thick. Remove from heat and set aside. Step 2: Make the Jamun Balls Mix Ingredients: In a bowl, crumble the khoya. Add all-purpose flour and baking soda. Mix well. Knead the Dough: Add milk gradually and knead until a soft dough forms. The dough should be smooth and not sticky. Shape the Balls: Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls (about the size of a marble). Ensure there are no cracks. Step 3: Fry the Gulab Jamun Heat Oil/Ghee: In a deep frying pan, heat oil or ghee over low to medium heat. It’s important that the oil is not too hot; otherwise, the balls will brown too quickly and remain uncooked inside. Fry the Balls: Gently drop the balls into the hot oil. Fry them slowly, stirring gently to ensure even cooking. They will expand as they cook. Fry until they turn golden brown. Drain: Once golden, remove them using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels. Step 4: Soak in Sugar Syrup Combine with Syrup: While the gulab jamuns are still warm, place them in the prepared sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 30 minutes to absorb the syrup. Step 5: Serve Serve the gulab jamun warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired. Enjoy your homemade gulab jamun!

  4. Paneer Butter Masala

    5. Heat butter in a pan, add onions, and sauté until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute. Add tomato puree, salt, and cook until oil separates. Add garam masala and mix well. Add paneer cubes and cream, and stir gently. Cook for 5 minutes, garnish with coriander, and serve hot.

  5. Chole Bhature

    6. Cook soaked chickpeas in a pressure cooker until soft. In a pan, heat oil, add onions, and sauté until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and chole masala; cook well. Add cooked chickpeas and water; simmer for 15 minutes. For bhature, mix flour, yogurt, and water to knead a dough; let it rest. Roll out bhature and deep fry until golden. Serve with chole.
